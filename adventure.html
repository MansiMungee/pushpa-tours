White-water Rafting & Kayaking

Experience the power of Himalayan rivers such as the Siang, Kameng, Teesta, Rangit and Jia Bhoroli. Depending on season and operator availability, routes can range from gentler river journeys to more demanding rafting experiences.

Caving in Meghalaya

Meghalaya is famous for its limestone cave systems, stalactites, stalagmites and underground landscapes. Caving routes can be designed for curious beginners or more adventurous travellers with suitable local guidance.

High-altitude Trekking

Explore alpine trails, mountain roads and snow-clad landscapes around Tawang, Dirang, Sela Pass, Mechuka and other Himalayan regions. These routes are best planned carefully around weather, access and fitness.

Living Root Bridge Walks

Walk through Meghalaya’s rainforest villages, waterfalls and living root bridge landscapes. These journeys combine hiking, indigenous knowledge, forest ecology and some of the most memorable scenery in Northeast India.

Angling & River Camps

Rivers such as Jia Bhoroli, Subansiri, Kapili and Manas are known for angling experiences, including Golden Mahseer in suitable areas and seasons. River camps can combine angling, birding, rafting and local cuisine.

Paragliding, Rock Climbing & Soft Adventure

For travellers seeking something different, we can help connect suitable activities such as paragliding, rock climbing, horse riding, kayaking, camping and gentle family-friendly adventure add-ons where available.

Adventure landscapes we can build into your journey

Each state offers a distinct kind of outdoor experience. We can focus on one region or combine several into a broader Northeast India adventure circuit.

Arunachal Pradesh

Rafting, mountain roads and Himalayan trails

Arunachal Pradesh is ideal for travellers who love dramatic terrain: river valleys, forest roads, high passes and alpine landscapes. The Siang and Kameng river systems are known for powerful rafting possibilities, while Tawang, Dirang, Anini and Mechuka offer mountain routes, village stays and high-altitude scenery.

Meghalaya

Caves, waterfalls, rainforests and root bridges

Meghalaya is one of the most rewarding soft-adventure destinations in India. Walk through rainforest trails, visit living root bridges, explore cave systems, swim near clear waterfalls, and experience landscapes shaped by rain, limestone, rivers and indigenous knowledge.

Sikkim & Assam

River sports, tea country and Himalayan adventure

Sikkim offers mountain roads, Teesta and Rangit river adventures, high-altitude landscapes and paragliding possibilities around Gangtok and surrounding areas. Assam adds river islands, tea gardens, camping, angling, kayaking, parasailing possibilities and accessible adventure near Guwahati, Nameri and Dibru-Saikhowa.

Nagaland & Manipur

Offbeat treks and floating landscapes

Dzukou Valley is known for rolling meadows, seasonal flowers and memorable trekking routes, while Manipur’s Loktak Lake offers one of Northeast India’s most unusual landscapes, with floating phumdis and the nearby Keibul Lamjao floating national park.
